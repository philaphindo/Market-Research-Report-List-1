<p><strong>The Compound Semiconductor Materials Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>III-Vs</li><li>II-VIs</li><li>IV-IVs</li><li>Sapphire</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Compound semiconductor materials are categorized based on their elemental groups. III-Vs (e.g., GaAs, InP) combine Group III and V elements, offering high electron mobility and efficiency, ideal for optoelectronics and high-frequency devices. II-VIs (e.g., ZnSe, CdTe) pair Group II and VI elements, excelling in light-emitting and sensing applications. IV-IVs (e.g., SiC, SiGe) blend Group IV elements, providing thermal stability and power efficiency for electronics. Sapphire, a crystalline form of aluminum oxide, is widely used as a substrate for LEDs and semiconductors due to its durability and optical clarity.</p></p>
