<p><p>Oil sands, also known as tar sands, are a mixture of sand, water, clay, and bitumen—a dense, viscous form of crude oil. They are primarily found in Canada, Venezuela, and other regions with significant hydrocarbon deposits. Extracting oil from oil sands involves surface mining or in-situ methods, which are more complex and costly compared to conventional oil extraction. Despite the challenges, oil sands are a critical energy resource due to their vast reserves and contribution to global oil supply.</p><p>The Oil Sands Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14% during the forecast period. The market is also witnessing increased collaboration between governments and private companies to optimize resource recovery and minimize environmental impact. These factors are shaping the future of the oil sands industry, making it a key player in the global energy landscape.</p></p>
