<p><p>The ceramic cup market is categorized into three temperature ranges based on the firing process. Medium-fired ceramics (1000–1200°C) provide a balance of durability and aesthetic appeal, often used in functional tableware. High-fired ceramics (≥1200°C) are robust and resistant to thermal shock, making them ideal for professional and high-end applications. Each type serves distinct consumer needs, influencing market dynamics.</p></p>
