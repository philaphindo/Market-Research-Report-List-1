<p><p>Automatic lighting control sensors enhance energy efficiency and convenience in various applications. Photocell sensors detect ambient light levels, adjusting artificial lighting accordingly to optimize illumination and reduce energy consumption. They are ideal for outdoor lighting and daylight harvesting. Passive infrared sensors, on the other hand, monitor motion and heat emitted by people, activating lighting when movement is detected and turning it off when areas are unoccupied. This technology is commonly used in residential and commercial spaces for effective energy management.</p></p>
